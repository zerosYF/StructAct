3.  **Check for Intervening Causes:**
    *   Identify if a new, independent, reckless, or intentional act by another agent directly caused the outcome (e.g., a drunk driver crashing a car, a villain detonating a bomb).
    *   If a significant intervening cause exists, it typically breaks the chain of responsibility from the original agent. The original agent's action may have created an opportunity but is not the cause.

4.  **Determine Intentionality (For Questions on Purpose):**
    *   **Only apply this step if the question asks about *intent* or *intentionally* causing an outcome.**
    *   Identify the agent's primary goal or purpose.
    *   **Intended Outcome:** The outcome was the agent's direct goal or purpose.
    *   **Foreseen Side-Effect:** The agent knew the outcome would occur but was indifferent or merely accepted it as a consequence of achieving their real goal.
    *   An agent "intentionally causes" an outcome only if it was their purpose, not merely a foreseen side-effect.

5.  **Analyze for Double-Prevention (Apply Sparingly):**
    *   Only apply this framework if the structure is clear: a deliberate inaction prevented an event that would have prevented the outcome.
